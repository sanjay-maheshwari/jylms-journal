About the Journal / Aim & Scope

Journal of Yoga, Leadership and Management Studies (JYLMS) is an independent, peer-reviewed online journal devoted to advancing scholarship and professional practice at the intersection of yoga, leadership and management.

The journal aims to build a bridge between traditional yogic knowledge and contemporary theories of leadership, human resource development, organizational behaviour, and workplace wellbeing. JYLMS encourages contributions from academics, researchers, practitioners, yoga professionals and management consultants.

The core scope of the journal includes, but is not limited to:

  • Yoga-based models of leadership, decision-making and self-management
  • Impact of yoga, mindfulness and meditation on employee wellbeing and productivity
  • Spirituality at work, values-based leadership and ethical management
  • Yoga interventions in stress management, burnout prevention and resilience building
  • Human resource management practices integrating holistic health and wellness
  • Case studies from corporates, educational institutions and NGOs implementing yoga-based initiatives
  • Empirical and conceptual studies on organizational culture, engagement and performance influenced by yogic principles

The journal publishes original research articles, review papers, conceptual papers, case studies and reflective practice reports relevant to its scope.

Author Guidelines

The Journal of Yoga, Leadership and Management Studies welcomes original, unpublished work that is not under review elsewhere. Submissions may be research articles, review papers, conceptual papers, case studies or practice-based reports.

  • Language: English
  • File format: Microsoft Word (.docx) for submission; final version as PDF
  • Length: Typically 4,000–7,000 words including references
  • Abstract: 150–250 words with 4–6 keywords
  • Referencing style: APA (latest edition) or as specified by the editorial board
  • Font & spacing: Times New Roman, 12 pt, 1.5 line spacing
  • Plagiarism: Manuscripts must be free from plagiarism; authors are responsible for originality.

Manuscripts should be emailed to the Editor-in-Chief at the contact email provided below. All submissions undergo a review process before a publication decision is made.
